## [HIV vaccine elicits broadly neutralizing antibodies in primates](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10837-5) ⭐️ 10.0/10

A study published in Nature on June 30, 2026, demonstrates that a vaccination strategy successfully induces broadly neutralizing antibodies (bNAbs) against HIV in non-human primates, representing a major breakthrough in HIV vaccine development. This is a significant step toward an effective HIV vaccine, as eliciting bNAbs has been a long-standing challenge. If translated to humans, it could provide broad protection against diverse HIV strains, potentially curbing the global epidemic. The study used a germline-targeting priming strategy followed by sequential boosting with immunogens designed to guide B cell maturation toward bNAb production. The results show that the vaccine elicited antibodies targeting conserved epitopes on the HIV envelope.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 30, 00:00

**Background**: Broadly neutralizing antibodies (bNAbs) are antibodies that can neutralize multiple HIV strains by targeting conserved regions of the virus. Historically, inducing bNAbs through vaccination has been extremely difficult due to HIV's high mutation rate and immune evasion. Recent advances in germline-targeting immunogen design have shown promise in priming bNAb precursors in animal models and early human trials.

## [CRISPR Epigenome Editing: Next Frontier in Disease Treatment](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-02075-6) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Nature reports on the next frontier of CRISPR technology: editing the epigenome to treat disease, alongside updates on the world's fastest computer and life advice. Epigenome editing with CRISPR represents a major advancement beyond traditional gene editing, enabling reversible modulation of gene expression without altering DNA sequence, which could lead to safer and more flexible therapies for a range of diseases. Unlike gene editing, epigenome editing modifies epigenetic marks such as DNA methylation and histone modifications at specific loci without introducing DNA breaks or relying on DNA repair pathways. The technology uses engineered DNA-binding domains like CRISPR-dCas9 fused with effector domains.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 29, 00:00

**Background**: Epigenome editing is a type of genetic engineering that targets specific sites to modify epigenetic features, influencing gene expression without changing the DNA sequence itself. It leverages tools like zinc finger proteins, TALEs, or CRISPR-dCas9 fusions to deliver effector domains that alter DNA methylation or histone modifications. This approach is considered safer than traditional gene editing because it avoids permanent changes to the genome and reduces the risk of off-target effects.
