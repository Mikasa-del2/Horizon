## [Base editing reveals NANOG essential for human embryogenesis](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10792-1) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A study published in Nature on June 25, 2026, used base editing to demonstrate that the transcription factor NANOG is essential for human embryogenesis. This is the first direct functional evidence of NANOG's necessity in early human development. This finding advances our understanding of human developmental biology and could inform regenerative medicine and infertility treatments. It also showcases the power of base editing for studying gene function in human embryos with high precision. Base editing enables single-nucleotide changes without double-strand breaks, reducing off-target effects. The study specifically disrupted NANOG in human embryos and observed developmental arrest, confirming its essential role.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 25, 00:00

**Background**: Base editing is a genome editing technology that combines CRISPR-Cas9 with a deaminase enzyme to precisely change one DNA base to another without cutting both strands. NANOG is a transcription factor known to maintain pluripotency in embryonic stem cells, but its role in intact human embryos was unclear. Human embryogenesis involves complex gene regulation, and studying essential genes in embryos has been challenging due to technical and ethical constraints.

## [China's LineShine tops global supercomputer ranking](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-02047-w) ⭐️ 9.0/10

China's LineShine supercomputer, hosted at the National Supercomputing Centre in Shenzhen, has achieved first place in the TOP500 ranking with a performance of 2.198 exaflops, surpassing 2 quintillion calculations per second. It is the first exascale system built entirely with CPUs and without US chips. This milestone demonstrates China's ability to achieve world-leading supercomputing performance using domestically designed chips, bypassing US export controls. It shifts the global HPC landscape and underscores the growing technological competition between the US and China. LineShine is based on the LingKun architecture using LX2 CPUs, presumably designed by Huawei, and consists of 47,000 CPUs. It is an all-CPU machine, unlike most top supercomputers that rely on GPUs from Nvidia or AMD.

**Background**: Supercomputers are measured in exaflops, where 1 exaflop equals 1 quintillion calculations per second. The TOP500 list ranks the world's fastest supercomputers. ## [Medical AI Models Vulnerable to Disparate Privacy Risks](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10688-0) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A study published in Nature on June 24, 2026, reveals that medical AI models are susceptible to membership inference attacks, which can determine whether a specific patient's data was used in training, leading to disparate privacy risks across different patient groups. This finding is critical because it highlights that medical AI, which relies on sensitive patient data, may inadvertently expose individuals to privacy breaches, especially for underrepresented groups, potentially undermining trust in healthcare AI and hindering its adoption. Membership inference attacks work by querying a trained model to infer if a specific data point was part of its training set, and the study shows that these attacks can have disparate impacts, meaning some patient subgroups are more vulnerable than others.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 24, 00:00

**Background**: Membership inference attacks are a type of privacy attack in machine learning where an adversary, given black-box access to a model, attempts to determine whether a particular record was used in training. This poses serious risks for data leakage, especially in medical contexts where patient data is highly sensitive. The concept of disparate privacy risks refers to the unequal distribution of privacy vulnerabilities across different demographic or clinical groups, which can exacerbate existing health disparities.

## [Optical Cooling via Charge Transfer in 2D Heterostructures](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10662-w) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers demonstrated optical cooling in two-dimensional semiconductor heterostructures through phonon-assisted interfacial charge transfer, achieving cryogen-free thermal management without stringent quantum-efficiency requirements. The study was published in Nature on June 24, 2026. This breakthrough relaxes the stringent quantum-efficiency constraints of traditional optical cooling, enabling practical cryogen-free thermal management for nanoscale devices. It could revolutionize thermal management in electronics, photonics, and quantum technologies by providing a compact, solid-state cooling method. The mechanism relies on phonon-assisted interfacial charge transfer in 2D heterostructures, which allows cooling without the need for near-unity quantum efficiency typically required in anti-Stokes fluorescence cooling. The work demonstrates a new physical principle for optical refrigeration.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 24, 00:00

**Background**: Optical cooling, or laser cooling of solids, traditionally relies on anti-Stokes fluorescence where a material emits photons at higher energies than it absorbs, requiring extremely high quantum efficiency (>99%) to overcome heating. Two-dimensional materials and their heterostructures offer unique electronic and phononic properties that can enable new cooling mechanisms. Phonon-assisted interfacial charge transfer involves the transfer of charge carriers across a heterojunction with the assistance of phonons, which can extract heat from the lattice.

## [Gigaparsec-scale anisotropic structures detected](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10702-5) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Using a novel parameter-free method called Angular Distribution of Pairwise Distances, researchers have found evidence for coherent anisotropic structures extending over gigaparsec scales, challenging the assumption that the Universe becomes statistically isotropic on large scales. This discovery directly challenges the cosmological principle, a foundational assumption in modern cosmology that underpins the standard ΛCDM model. If confirmed, it could require a fundamental revision of our understanding of the Universe's large-scale structure and evolution. The study, published in Nature on June 24, 2026, uses a parameter-free method to measure directional correlations in cosmic structures. The detected anisotropies are on gigaparsec scales, far exceeding the expected scale of homogeneity (around 100 Mpc).

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 24, 00:00

**Background**: The cosmological principle states that on sufficiently large scales, the Universe is homogeneous and isotropic. This principle is a cornerstone of the standard Big Bang model and is supported by observations of the cosmic microwave background. However, recent discoveries of large structures like the Hercules-Corona Borealis Great Wall have hinted at possible violations. The new study provides a systematic, model-independent test using a novel statistical method.

## [Half-Life 2 Playable in Browser via WebAssembly](https://hl2.slqnt.dev/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Half-Life 2 has been ported to run directly in a web browser using WebAssembly, as demonstrated at hl2.slqnt.dev. This allows the full game to be played without installation or a Steam client. This achievement showcases the growing capability of WebAssembly to run complex, AAA-quality games in the browser, potentially lowering barriers to access and enabling new distribution models. It also highlights how legacy games can remain playable on modern systems that no longer support their original binaries. The port appears to be based on the original Windows executable compiled via Emscripten, but some shaders (e.g., character eyes) are missing, resulting in less accurate rendering compared to native. The project is open-source, with a blog post detailing the technical process.

hackernews · panza · Jun 25, 06:00 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48669534)

**Background**: WebAssembly (Wasm) is a low-level binary instruction format that runs in modern browsers at near-native speed. It allows developers to port existing C/C++ games to the web using tools like Emscripten. Previous notable ports include Doom 3 and Quake 3, demonstrating the feasibility of running demanding games in a browser.
