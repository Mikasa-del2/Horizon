## [Quantinuum Helios: 98-qubit trapped-ion quantum computer](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10676-4) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Quantinuum has unveiled Helios, a 98-qubit trapped-ion quantum processor based on the QCCD architecture, which demonstrates performance beyond classical capabilities and provides a clear path for scaling. This breakthrough shows that trapped-ion quantum computers with all-to-all connectivity can outperform classical computers, marking a significant milestone in quantum computing and paving the way for practical quantum advantage. Helios uses 137Ba+ hyperfine qubits and achieves all-to-all connectivity via a rotatable ion storage ring connecting two quantum operation regions through a junction, enabling arbitrary qubit pairs to interact with high fidelity.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 17, 00:00

**Background**: Trapped-ion quantum computers use individual ions as qubits, manipulated by lasers or microwaves. The QCCD (quantum charge-coupled device) architecture shuttles ions between different trap zones to enable modular scaling and all-to-all connectivity, which is crucial for complex algorithms.

This finding provides direct experimental evidence for the role of signaling pathways in the evolution of multicellularity, potentially reshaping our understanding of early animal development and the origin of metazoan body plans. The study used the ctenophore Mnemiopsis leidyi and the sea anemone Nematostella vectensis, comparing organizer activity. rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 17, 00:00

The blastoporal organizer is a group of cells that directs embryonic development and body axis formation, previously known mainly in bilaterians and cnidarians. The Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway is a conserved pathway involved in cell fate determination and axis formation.

An investigation uncovered over 10,000 GitHub repositories distributing Trojan malware, exploiting automated agents and dependency confusion to infect systems. The attackers target automated agents rather than humans, frequently updating repositories to appear in search results, and use dependency confusion to trick package managers into fetching malicious packages.

**Background**: Dependency confusion attacks occur when package managers mistakenly download a malicious public package instead of a legitimate internal one with the same name. Automated agents, such as CI/CD pipelines and AI tools, often fetch dependencies without human review, making them prime targets.
