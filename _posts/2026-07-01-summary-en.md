The record efficiency of 22.0% EQE brings perovskite LEDs closer to commercial viability. The isomeric multi-hydrogen-bonding approach uses isomeric molecules to form hydrogen-bonding networks that passivate defects and enhance charge balance. The blue PeLEDs achieve saturated emission with high stability, though the exact operational lifetime is not specified in the summary.

## [2 nm carrier transfer length measured in 2D transistors](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10707-0) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers used cross-sectional scanning tunnelling microscopy to directly measure a 2 nm carrier transfer length in bismuth-contacted monolayer MoS2 transistors, as published in Nature on July 1, 2026. This measurement defines the metal-contact scaling limits for sub-10 nm 2D electronic devices, providing a critical benchmark for future nanoelectronics and semiconductor design. The transfer length of 2 nm is significantly shorter than typical values in conventional 3D/2D metal-semiconductor junctions (often >50 nm), highlighting the advantage of bismuth contacts for 2D materials.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jul 1, 00:00

**Background**: In transistors, the carrier transfer length (or transfer length) is the distance over which charge carriers move from the metal contact into the semiconductor channel. It is a key parameter for contact resistance and device scaling. The transfer length method (TLM) is commonly used to extract this value, but direct imaging has been challenging. This study uses cross-sectional scanning tunnelling microscopy (XSTM) to visualize the carrier distribution at the atomic scale.

## [JWST Detects Hydrocarbons and Aerosols on White Dwarf Planet](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10514-7) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Using JWST transmission spectroscopy, astronomers have detected hydrocarbons and aerosols in the atmosphere of WD 1856 b, the first intact planet confirmed orbiting a white dwarf. This discovery provides unprecedented insights into the atmospheric chemistry and thermal history of a planet that survived the red giant phase of its star, offering clues about planetary evolution in extreme environments. The planet WD 1856 b is a Jupiter-like giant with a mass no more than six times that of Jupiter, and it orbits within the white dwarf's 'forbidden zone,' where planets would normally be engulfed.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jul 1, 00:00

**Background**: White dwarfs are the dense remnants of Sun-like stars after they exhaust their nuclear fuel and shed their outer layers. During the red giant phase, planets in close orbits are typically engulfed, making the survival of WD 1856 b surprising. Transmission spectroscopy analyzes starlight filtered through a planet's atmosphere during a transit, revealing its chemical composition.

## [N4-Acetylcytidine boosts synthetic mRNA yield and fidelity](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10729-8) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A study published in Nature on July 1, 2026, demonstrates that N4-acetylcytidine (ac4C) modification enhances both translation yield and fidelity of synthetic mRNA, outperforming the current industry standard N1-methylpseudouridine (m1Ψ). This discovery could lead to more effective and safer mRNA therapeutics and vaccines, as ac4C improves both the amount and accuracy of protein production from synthetic mRNA, potentially reducing side effects and increasing efficacy. The study found that different RNA modifications, including ac4C and m1Ψ, elicit different translation elongation rates, which in turn affect translation output and fidelity. ac4C showed superior performance in both yield and accuracy compared to m1Ψ.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jul 1, 00:00

**Background**: Synthetic mRNA is used in vaccines and therapeutics, but its efficacy depends on efficient and accurate translation into proteins. N1-methylpseudouridine (m1Ψ) is currently the standard modification used in approved mRNA vaccines like those from Pfizer-BioNTech and Moderna. N4-acetylcytidine (ac4C) is a naturally occurring modification found in tRNAs, rRNAs, and mRNAs across all domains of life.
