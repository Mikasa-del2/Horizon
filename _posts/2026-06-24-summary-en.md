## [GW250114 Reveals Post-Merger Black Hole Horizon Signatures](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10696-0) ⭐️ 10.0/10

The gravitational wave event GW250114, observed after the merger of two black holes, has revealed direct signatures of the remnant black hole's horizon, including frame-dragging effects and horizon surface gravity. This is the first direct observational evidence of post-merger black hole horizon signatures, enabling a new way to test general relativity in strong-field regimes and measure fundamental black hole properties. The observation detected a direct wave after merger, which allowed measurement of frame-dragging within the ergosphere and probing of the horizon's surface gravity, as reported in Nature on June 24, 2026.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 24, 00:00

**Background**: Frame-dragging is a phenomenon predicted by general relativity where a rotating black hole twists spacetime around it, especially within the ergosphere. Horizon surface gravity is a measure of the gravitational acceleration at the event horizon. Gravitational waves from black hole mergers provide a unique laboratory to study these effects.

## [Medical AI faces disparate privacy risks from membership inference](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10688-0) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A Nature study published on June 24, 2026 demonstrates that medical AI models are highly vulnerable to membership inference attacks, which can determine whether a specific patient's data was used in training, with heightened risks for underrepresented groups. This research exposes a critical privacy vulnerability in medical AI, potentially undermining patient trust and hindering the adoption of AI in healthcare. The disparate impact on underrepresented groups raises serious equity concerns. The study found that attack success increases with model capacity, and that patients from underrepresented groups—stratified by disease status, race, insurance, sex, or imaging protocol—face higher privacy risks. The findings suggest that current privacy protections may be inadequate.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 24, 00:00

**Background**: Membership inference attacks (MIAs) are a type of privacy breach where an adversary queries an AI model to determine if a specific data record was part of the training dataset. Unlike traditional data breaches, MIAs exploit the model's behavior rather than stealing files directly. Medical AI models are trained on sensitive patient data, making privacy protection critical.

A Nature review published on June 24, 2026, details the immunological mechanisms of mRNA-lipid-nanoparticle vaccines and describes how platform components can be modified to fine-tune immune responses against challenging pathogens. This review provides a comprehensive framework for rationally designing next-generation mRNA vaccines, which is critical for pandemic preparedness and developing vaccines against difficult pathogens like HIV, tuberculosis, and cancer. The review discusses how lipid nanoparticle components, mRNA modifications, and delivery routes influence innate and adaptive immunity, and highlights strategies such as hepatocyte detargeting to enhance T cell responses.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 24, 00:00

**Background**: mRNA vaccines use lipid nanoparticles (LNPs) to deliver mRNA encoding an antigen, which is then expressed by host cells to trigger an immune response. The LNP itself can act as an adjuvant, and the cell types expressing the antigen (e.g., myocytes vs. hepatocytes) can modulate the resulting immunity. Recent studies have shown that mRNA expression in hepatocytes can suppress T cell responses via the PD1/PDL1 pathway, while expression in myocytes enhances immunity.

Researchers demonstrated optical cooling in two-dimensional semiconductor heterostructures through phonon-assisted interfacial charge transfer, as published in Nature on June 24, 2026. This breakthrough enables cryogen-free thermal management without the stringent quantum-efficiency requirements of traditional optical cooling, potentially revolutionizing thermal engineering in nanoscale devices. The mechanism relies on phonon-assisted charge transfer at the interface of 2D heterostructures, which occurs on ultrafast timescales (~100 fs) despite weak interlayer coupling and momentum mismatch.

**Background**: Optical cooling typically requires materials with near-unity quantum efficiency to achieve net cooling via anti-Stokes fluorescence. This new approach relaxes that requirement by using interfacial charge transfer to extract heat, making it applicable to a wider range of materials.

## [Gigaparsec-scale anisotropic structures challenge cosmological principle](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10702-5) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new study published in Nature on June 24, 2026, reports the detection of coherent anisotropic structures extending over gigaparsec scales using a parameter-free method called the Angular Distribution of Pairwise Distances. This finding challenges the long-held assumption that the Universe becomes statistically isotropic on sufficiently large scales. If confirmed, this discovery would represent a paradigm shift in cosmology, undermining the cosmological principle that underpins the standard model of the Universe. It could have profound implications for our understanding of cosmic inflation, dark energy, and the large-scale structure of the cosmos. The study introduces the Angular Distribution of Pairwise Distances (ADPD) as a novel, parameter-free statistical tool for measuring directional correlations in galaxy surveys. The detected anisotropic structures span gigaparsec scales, which are far larger than the scales at which the Universe was previously thought to become isotropic.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 24, 00:00

**Background**: The cosmological principle states that on large scales, the Universe is homogeneous and isotropic—meaning it looks the same everywhere and in every direction. This principle is a foundational assumption of the standard ΛCDM model of cosmology. Previous studies have found hints of large-scale anomalies, such as the CMB dipole and quadrupole, but none have definitively shown coherent structures on gigaparsec scales.

## [Founding a GmbH in Germany: €9600, 152 Days, No Invoice Yet](https://paolino.me/founding-a-company-in-germany/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A founder recounts spending €9600 and 152 days to establish a GmbH in Germany, only to remain unable to send invoices due to bureaucratic delays. This story highlights the severe bureaucratic hurdles in Germany's company formation process, which can deter entrepreneurs and stifle startup growth. The author opted for a GmbH with €25,000 minimum capital, but faced delays in notary appointments, trade office registration, and tax ID issuance, ultimately costing €9600 before generating any revenue.

hackernews · earcar · Jun 24, 12:31 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48658718)

**Background**: In Germany, a GmbH (Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung) is a limited liability company requiring at least €25,000 in share capital. The formation process involves notarization, commercial register entry, and tax registration, which can take months. An alternative is the UG (Unternehmergesellschaft), a cheaper variant with lower capital requirements, but it may be perceived as less credible by clients.

**Discussion**: Commenters debate the necessity of the €25,000 minimum capital, with some arguing it protects creditors while others see it as an unnecessary barrier. There is also discussion about the UG's stigma and alternative legal structures like sole proprietorships.
