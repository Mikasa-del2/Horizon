## [Stem cell therapy gives 15-year remission for autoimmune disease](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01925-7) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Two patients with neuromyelitis optica spectrum disorder (NMOSD) achieved 15-year remission after receiving autologous stem cell transplant therapy, as reported in Nature on June 19, 2026. This is the first long-term efficacy data for stem cell therapy in NMOSD, a severe autoimmune disease that damages the spinal cord and optic nerve, offering hope for durable remission without ongoing immunosuppression. The therapy used the patients' own blood-forming stem cells (autologous stem cell transplant) to reset the immune system. Both patients remained relapse-free and off immunosuppressive drugs for 15 years.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 19, 00:00

**Background**: Neuromyelitis optica spectrum disorder (NMOSD) is an autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks the optic nerve and spinal cord, causing blindness, paralysis, and other severe symptoms. Autologous stem cell transplant (ASCT) involves harvesting a patient's own stem cells, then using chemotherapy to deplete the faulty immune system before reinfusing the stem cells to rebuild a healthy one.

## [Pollen Diet Helps Heliconius Butterflies Age Gracefully](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01948-0) ⭐️ 4.0/10

A new study published in Nature reveals that Heliconius butterflies live longer than other butterflies due to their unique pollen-rich diet. This finding provides insights into the mechanisms of aging and could inspire research into dietary interventions for promoting longevity in other species, including humans. Heliconius butterflies are among the longest-lived butterfly species, and their pollen consumption is linked to reduced oxidative damage and enhanced cellular maintenance.

rss · Nature - Latest Research · Jun 19, 00:00

**Background**: Most butterflies feed on nectar, which provides energy but lacks protein. Heliconius butterflies uniquely collect and digest pollen, obtaining amino acids that support tissue repair and antioxidant defenses. This dietary adaptation is thought to slow aging processes.
